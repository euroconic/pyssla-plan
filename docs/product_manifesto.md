# The "Healthy Kid" Manifesto 🍎

**Core Philosophy:** We build **Confidence**, not Addiction.
*   **❌ No Casino:** No "Daily Streaks", "Loot Boxes", or meaningless "Login Rewards".
*   **✅ Healthy Creativity:** We reward **Effort** and **Completion**.
*   **✅ Intrinsic Motivation:** The reward is the *Art itself*, not a digital badge.

## 📜 The 4 Golden Rules

### 1. The "Finish Line" Rule 🏁
*   **Principle:** Starting is easy; finishing is hard. We scaffold the *end* of the process.
*   **Implementation:** When a child reaches 80% completion, we cheer them on. We don't verify "Did you log in?", we verify "Did you finish the heart?".

### 2. The "Effort" Rule 💪
*   **Principle:** Praise the *process*, not the person. (Growth Mindset).
*   **Bad:** "You are smart!" (Fixed mindset).
*   **Good:** "You worked hard on those red beads!" (Growth mindset).

### 3. The "Anti-Frustration" Rule 🛡️
*   **Principle:** Pyssla is physically hard. We must prevent rage-quitting.
*   **Implementation:** If a child mistakenly clears the board, we possess an **Undo** button that saves the day. If they pause for too long, we offer help.

### 4. The "Pride" Rule 🦁
*   **Principle:** Digital art must become physical reality to feel "real".
*   **Implementation:** The UI always pushes towards **"Print & Build"**. The screen is just a blueprint; the goal is the real-world object.

## 🧭 Guidance for Parents & Kids: "Why Finish?"

We don't use a robot to nag. We provide **Wisdom** to the parent/child team.

### The Core Message
*   **For Kids:** "Every finished project makes your maker-muscle stronger."
*   **For Parents:** "The goal isn't the bead art. The goal is the *feeling* of completion."

### The "Small Steps" Technique 🪜
We encourage breaking the board into zones:
1.  **The Outline** (Skeleton)
2.  **The Fill** (Muscle)
3.  **The Ironing** (Skin)
